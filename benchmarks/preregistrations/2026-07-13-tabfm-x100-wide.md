# Statement of intended research: TabFM's best arm at full scale

Filed 2026-07-13, before any result of this study exists.

## Selection disclosure

The arm under test, `clfx100`, is chosen BECAUSE it was the best of
eleven in the pre-registered wide study (median gap 0.39 nats to
laplace, the narrowest). Selecting the winner of a previous comparison
induces a selection effect, so the honest prior is regression toward the
pack (the other classifier arms sat at 0.5 to 0.8). We also state what
the arm is, since its name misleads: clfx100 is not a binning variant
but the scale-invariance probe, clf8 applied to the series multiplied by
100 and scored back through the exact affine change of variables. Its
advantage means the pipeline is not scale-invariant and the rescaling
flatters TabFM's internal treatment of magnitudes.

## Questions

1. Does clfx100's 0.39-nat median gap persist on the full qualifying
   universe (921 series, the prophet-wide effective universe, frozen at
   `tabfm_x100_universe.txt` with the length screen applied at selection
   so the selector/runner mismatch of the prophet study cannot recur)?
2. Sandwiched (clfx100 machinery on 100x the parade z, exact affine and
   Jacobian accounting), does TabFM's epsilon match the ~-0.02 nats seen
   for its regressor arm and for Prophet?

## Frozen analysis plan

Identical to `2026-07-13-prophet-wide.md`: per-series median gap and win
rate, unweighted AND family-weighted (one vote per family), strata table
by rho and repeat bins, failures reported with reasons, no pooling.
Scoring is the wide study's protocol unchanged: TEST=150, logpdf floored
at -20 identically for every method. Expectation stated in advance: Q1
gap regresses upward from 0.39 but laplace remains ahead on the
family-weighted view; Q2 epsilon lands in [-0.06, +0.01].

## Deviations

- Run start 2026-07-13 evening (Mac Studio, mps, torch 2.4.1, 17-18s per
  series). 921/921 scored, zero failures.
- The Q2 criterion band was written without naming a weighting. The
  unweighted epsilon meets it; the family-weighted epsilon lands far
  OUTSIDE it on the positive side (+0.87). Diagnosis below, as the plan
  requires for out-of-band results.

## Results (first read, per the frozen plan)

Q1, raw clfx100: median -0.155 unweighted (laplace wins 76%), -0.220
family-weighted (68% of families). The selection-effect regression we
pre-stated as the prior did NOT materialize; the arm's gap narrowed from
0.39 on the stratified 226 to 0.15 here. Notably raw clfx100 WINS the
repeat stratum outright (+0.35 median over laplace, n=181).

Q2, sandwiched clfx100: unweighted median -0.020 (44% wins), inside the
pre-stated band and indistinguishable from Prophet's and the regressor
arm's epsilon. Family-weighted: +0.87 with 68% of families positive,
driven by repeat-heavy (+1.99, n=181) and rho~0 (+0.93, n=236) strata.

Diagnosis of the out-of-band result: fine binning on 100x the parade z
is a DISCRETE density head. On lattice-heavy series the z-coordinates
inherit atom structure, the binned classifier models those atoms
directly, and it finds roughly two nats that laplace's continuous
predictive leaves on the table there. This is the first challenger
configuration to beat laplace anywhere at scale, it does so exactly
where every Gaussian-head challenger collapsed, and it revises the
sandwich section's residual-test bound: a few hundredths of a nat on
continuous series, but material recoverable structure on repeat-heavy
series for a discrete inner model. The constructive implication for the
library is an atom-aware z-space head or a sharper lattice treatment.
