# Statement of intended research: TabFM vs laplace

Filed 2026-07-09, 17:30 EDT. Harness: `benchmarks/tabfm_study.py`, committed
with this statement. The full run started earlier the same day and was in
progress at filing; the disclosure section below lists everything observed
before this document was committed.

Public record: the pre-registration was announced the same evening, while
the run was in progress, on
[LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/microprediction_introducing-tabfm-a-zero-shot-foundation-activity-7481104107668348928-u-6N),
giving the statement a timestamp independent of this repository's history.

## Research question

Google released TabFM on 2026-06-30, a 1.64B-parameter zero-shot foundation
model for tabular classification and regression. It is not a forecaster.
The question: when a change series is presented to TabFM through the standard
autoregressive tabular reduction, does its in-context learning carry any
distributional forecasting signal, and how does it compare to `laplace` under
the same protocol already applied to TimesFM, Chronos-Bolt, Moirai and
Lag-Llama?

## Hypotheses (outcome-neutral)

- H1: on the continuous split, the per-series log-likelihood win rate of
  `laplace` over the TabFM reconstruction differs from 50%.
- H2: same, for CRPS.
- H3: on the MAE undercard (TabFM's regressor vs the median of `laplace`'s
  predictive), the per-series win rate differs from 50%.

No direction is asserted. Both directions are publishable here and will be
published identically.

## Exact setup

Everything below is frozen as the harness defaults in the same commit.

**Universe.** The identical universe to the prior foundation-model study:
the first 120 cached FRED series (sorted by id) whose change series has at
least 1000 observations, taking the last 1000 changes of each. The cache is
`benchmarks/data`. No series is added, dropped or substituted after filing.

**Protocol.** Rolling one-step-ahead over the last TEST=150 changes of each
series, zero-shot, context CTX=256 preceding changes, exactly as for the
other foundation models. `laplace` is re-run on the identical series and
windows.

**Reduction.** Each context is arranged as a table: 248 training rows whose
features are LAGS=8 consecutive changes and whose target is the next change;
the test row is the 8 most recent changes. The in-context table is refreshed
every STRIDE=10 steps (test-row features are current at every step); the
TabFM ensemble is trimmed to NE=4 members from the default 32. Both
concessions exist to make 1.64B-parameter CPU inference feasible and are
fixed ex ante.

**Density reconstruction (ours, not Google's).** TabFM's regression head
emits a point, so the distributional comparison runs through its classifier:
training targets are binned by deciles into at most 10 classes (10 is the
model's `max_classes` ceiling; duplicate quantile edges are collapsed), and
the predicted class probabilities become a bar distribution smoothed into
the same `Dist` as every other challenger: one Gaussian per bin at the bin
centre, bandwidth 0.29 times the bin width (the moment match for a uniform
bin), probability floor 1e-6 per bin. Degenerate constant windows fall back
to an atom at the training median with bandwidth 1e-9, the same convention
the sample- and quantile-reconstructions already apply to the other models.

**Scoring.** Held-out log-likelihood and CRPS through the same `Dist` code
as all prior studies, non-finite logpdf floored at -20 (the existing
protocol constant). Splits: continuous vs repeat-heavy at repeat fraction
0.05 over the test window, the pre-existing rule. Win rates are per-series,
reported for all series and for the continuous split.

**MAE undercard.** TabFM's regressor, used as designed on the same tables,
scored by MAE against the median (`quantile(0.5)`) of `laplace`'s
per-step predictive.

**Environment.** `tabfm` 1.0.0, torch 2.4.1, scikit-learn 1.9.0, CPU,
Python 3.11, weights snapshot `77cb9cc1b4fd3a9c77fbb9552c218200bb4dab83`
of `google/tabfm-1.0.0-pytorch` (safetensors converted once to
`pytorch_model.bin` because the pip loader and the HF repo disagree on
format; conversion is byte-preserving on tensors).

## Analysis plan

For each hypothesis, a two-sided exact binomial sign test at alpha 0.05 on
the per-series wins in the continuous split (H1, H2) and over all scored
series (H3). Ties count as half-wins split evenly. Point summaries: win
rates on both splits and the median per-series log-likelihood gap in nats.
No other tests, subgroups or metrics will be added after results are read;
anything exploratory will be labelled exploratory.

## Reporting commitments

All 120 rows are published in `benchmarks/results_foundation_tabfm.csv` and
`benchmarks/results_tabfm_mae.csv` regardless of outcome. If TabFM wins
anywhere, that is reported at the same volume as where it loses, following
the standing rule on this site (compare the GARCH-t price-series row, where
the belt is conceded). The Challengers page gets one row per outcome table;
any deep-dive page discloses the reconstruction and both concessions in its
fairness accounting.

## Observed before filing (full disclosure)

- A 4-series smoke test: three recession-dummy series (degenerate constant
  windows, both methods near-perfect, TabFM's atom fallback sharper:
  TabFM 19.80 vs laplace 18.27-18.74 nats) and AAA10Y (laplace ll 3.166 vs
  TabFM 2.071; CRPS 0.0113 vs 0.0114; MAE 0.0150 vs 0.0155).
- At filing, the classifier pass had completed 14 of 120 series. In the log,
  TabFM was ahead on log-likelihood on several early repeat-heavy
  commercial-paper series (e.g. AB4180AAAMT -7.232 vs -7.264). No summary
  statistic, no split-level number and no MAE row beyond the smoke test had
  been computed or read.
- Timing measurements used to pick STRIDE and NE (a synthetic 248x8 table
  and the smoke test), and one design observation from the synthetic check:
  the 10-bin probabilities looked unimodal and sensible. Nothing about
  relative accuracy on real series informed any parameter.
