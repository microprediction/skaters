Live forecasting race

The whole thing runs in your browser — the actual skaters JavaScript port against classical baselines, on 150 real FRED daily change-series (from the benchmark's popularity-ranked universe). No API key, no server. Every method is turned into the same predictive distribution and scored on held-out log-likelihood and CRPS, rolling one-step-ahead — the paper's harness, live.

1. Race the whole universe

The online methods (laplace and a naive Gaussian) sweep all 150 series. Watch the win-rate settle.

2. Watch one series — with the heavyweights

Add the refit-every-step baselines. laplace finishes in a blink; Holt-Winters, ARIMA, and augurs (ETS) grind through a fresh fit at every step. The slowness is the point.

ARIMA (npm arima) and augurs ETS (npm @bsull/augurs) are WebAssembly, loaded from a CDN on demand; if they can't load they're skipped. Every method is wrapped as the same Dist and scored identically — a point/interval forecast becomes a Gaussian, since that's all these emit.

Reproduce at scale (all 894 non-price series, more baselines): the benchmarks. Why log-likelihood is the score: metrics.
