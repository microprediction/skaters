Challengers

Models run against laplace under committed protocols, with the statements filed before the results existed.

Every study on this page follows the same rules: the universe is frozen and committed first, the harness and scoring are committed with it, a statement of intent is filed before any result is read, results land in the repository next to the code that produced them, and anything that went wrong is written in a deviations section rather than quietly dropped. The statements live in benchmarks/preregistrations.

Prophet

Prophet is the challenger we have measured most thoroughly, at three scales. (For the prehistory, see the 2021 critique; this page is the quantitative sequel on live protocols.)

Scale one: thirty series, five opponents. The Rosenblatt front-end study ran Prophet with real calendar dates, rolling refits, and exact change-of-variables accounting. Raw, it scored 1.64 mean log-likelihood against laplace's 3.67. Sandwiched (fed laplace's z stream and mapped back through the exact Jacobian) it reached 3.70, the largest median lift of the five opponents, and its calendar machinery added +0.03 nats over laplace alone, the largest epsilon any challenger has recorded.

Scale two: the 226-series stratified universe. On the pre-registered TabFM universe (five autocorrelation bins crossed with three repeat bins, family caps), raw Prophet trails by 1.91 nats median with 3 wins in 217, collapsing to −9.8 on repeat-heavy series, whose lattice its Gaussian head cannot express. Sandwiched, it lands 0.020 nats behind laplace. The +0.03 calendar surplus from scale one does not replicate here.

Scale three: every qualifying series. Pre-registered with a frozen 1,367-series universe, no family caps, and a frozen analysis plan including a family-weighted concentration check and a written success criterion. Results, per that plan:

Three details make these numbers quotable. The family weighting mattered: one FRED family (a job-postings panel) is 48% of the uncapped universe and flattered raw Prophet's unweighted median by nearly a factor of six; the frozen plan required both views. The sandwich epsilon is stable from 30 series to 921: Prophet fed laplace's coordinates always lands about 0.02 nats behind laplace itself, our strongest evidence that little conditional structure is left in the residuals. And the deviations section discloses a design error in full: a selector/runner length mismatch silently reduced the universe from 1,367 to 921 before any result existed.

TabFM

A tabular foundation model with 12 GB of weights, evaluated under a pre-registered eleven-arm study on the 226-series universe: laplace wins the likelihood race against every arm (71–88% of series), the best arm trailing by 0.39 nats median. Sandwiched, TabFM converges to 0.019 nats behind laplace; as a component supplying the conditional mean, it subtracts value. The paper's foundation-model section carries the full tables, the strata reading, and the deployed footprint comparison (12 GB and 45 ms per forecast on a GPU, against 988 KB and 0.3 ms on one CPU core).

Everyone else

Twelve classical and neural distributional baselines and four zero-shot foundation models are scored on the papers page under the same one-pass protocols. The sandwich result generalizes across them: fed laplace's z stream, every opponent tried so far converges to laplace plus or minus a few hundredths of a nat.
